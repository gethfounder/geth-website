01 — About

What is GETH?

GETH (Global Energy Trade Hub) is an idea-stage project designed to become a digital ecosystem for over-the-counter (OTC) commodity trading, addressing three pain points in the $4.6 trillion market: manual processes, certificate fraud, and slow dispute resolution.

Our approach: secure the legal foundation first (UAE VARA + DMCC licensing), then build an automated platform with AI-driven counterparty scoring, smart contract escrow, and real-time cargo tracking — then validate with test deals from the founder's industry network.

05 — Risks & Mitigations

What Could Go Wrong, and Our Plan

An honest look at the biggest open questions at this stage — and how we're addressing each one.

Risk

VARA declines or delays token/licensing approval

Regulatory timelines in a new framework are inherently uncertain.

Mitigation

ADGM fallback structure

Pre-scoped contingency plan to pivot to ADGM licensing or a non-token utility-points model.

Risk

No regulated bank signs the escrow partnership

Without a bank partner, automated fund locking cannot go live.

Mitigation

Multiple parallel conversations

Engaging more than one UAE bank in parallel; legal counsel structuring terms both banks would accept.

Risk

Pilot traders don't convert after MVP

Current interest is non-binding and expressed pre-MVP.

Mitigation

Manual-first validation

Running the first test deals manually, before the platform is built, to prove demand cheaply and early.

Risk

Two-person team is thin for the scope

Legal, platform, and business development all compete for founder time.

Mitigation

30% of round to team

Pre-seed use of funds allocates dedicated budget to hire before platform build begins.

Risk

Regulatory fragmentation from multi-jurisdiction expansion

Pursuing several licensing regimes at once (GCC, Hong Kong, Singapore) in parallel would strain a pre-seed budget and dilute focus.

Mitigation

One license first, expansion only post-PMF

UAE (VARA + DMCC) remains the sole legal home through MVP and test deals. Qatar's QFC is the natural next license given its 2024 Digital Assets Framework already permits commodity tokenization on common-law terms parallel to VARA — pursued only after UAE product-market fit.

08 — Legal Framework

Legal Framework & Compliance

GETH operates as a technology platform — not a trading house, carrier, or insurer. We provide the digital infrastructure connecting verified counterparties, regulated banks, licensed inspectors, and professional intermediaries within a compliant ecosystem.

Full legal structuring detail is in the Executive Summary PDF. Below is a condensed overview.

Platform Legal Status+

GETH is a digital intermediary — it does not buy, sell, transport, or insure commodities. All commercial transactions occur directly between verified counterparties.

  • Platform operator under UAE Electronic Transactions Law
  • No proprietary trading activity
  • No custody of physical assets
  • Regulated via VARA + DMCC licensing
  • English Common Law governance (DIFC)
Logistics Legal Framework+

GETH integrates with logistics providers through standardized legal agreements allocating responsibility and risk clearly.

  • Master Service Agreement with shipping companies
  • API Integration Agreement for booking & tracking
  • Electronic Bill of Lading (eBL) — legally recognized
  • Incoterms® 2020 guidance via AI assistant
  • Force majeure & dispute clauses
Insurance Legal Framework+

GETH acts as an insurance aggregator and facilitator — policies are issued by licensed insurers via API integration.

  • Brokerage Agreement with Lloyd's Coverholders
  • Smart Insurance Policy with automated triggers
  • Institute Cargo Clauses (A/B/C) coverage
  • NFT Insurance Certificate as proof of coverage
  • Automated claims processing
Smart Contracts as Legal Instruments+

Smart contracts on GETH are designed to be legally binding under UAE and international law, with immutable audit trails.

  • UAE Federal Law No. 1 of 2022
  • ADGM Smart Transaction Regulations 2021
  • DIFC Digital Assets Law 2023
  • UNCITRAL Model Law compliance
  • Qualified Electronic Signatures
Compliance & Regulatory+

"Compliance by Design" — embedded into every workflow rather than added afterward.

  • AML/CFT under UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 20 of 2018
  • KYB/KYC 4-level verification
  • Sanctions screening (OFAC, EU, UN, HMT)
  • PEP monitoring
  • UAE PDPL + GDPR compliance
Digital Documentation+

Every transaction generates legally significant documents stored on-chain with immutable audit trails.

  • Commercial Invoice (hash on-chain)
  • Electronic Bill of Lading (NFT)
  • Certificate of Origin (NFT)
  • Insurance & Inspection Certificates (NFT)
  • Smart Contract, on-chain & immutable

Deal Flow Vision

An illustrative example based on real fuel oil trades in the founder's network.

Transaction Overview (Target)

Seller

UAE-based oil refining company exporting 50,000 MT fuel oil.

Buyer

Indian energy company requiring fuel oil for power generation.

Intermediary (optional)

Brokerage firm originating the deal. Buyers and sellers can also connect directly via GETH's AI matching, without an intermediary.

Lot Creation & Verification

Seller uploads the fuel oil specification and SGS quality certificate. AI verifies seller credentials, financial standing, and trading history.

Target: counterparty risk scored in <10 minutes

AI Matching & Discovery

GETH's engine matches buyer requirements with available lots. If an intermediary is involved, they receive a pre-calculated commission. Otherwise buyer and seller connect directly.

Target: automated deal discovery — no cold calls

Escrow & Contract Lock

Buyer deposits $19M into a regulated escrow account. Smart contract locks price, quality specs, delivery timeline, and commission.

Target: 1.10% all-in fee ($209,000)

Logistics Coordination

Seller confirms cargo readiness; buyer selects shipping route. IoT sensors activated for real-time tracking.

Target: transparent logistics for all parties

Real-Time Cargo Tracking

IoT devices monitor temperature, humidity, and location throughout the voyage, streamed to GETH's platform.

Target: tamper-proof tracking data

Quality Verification

On arrival, an SGS or Bureau Veritas inspector checks cargo against the NFT certificate. Results are sent via secure API; the smart contract cross-verifies against original terms.

Target: instant confirmation — no 18-month disputes

Automated Settlement

Smart contract distributes payment via bank webhooks: $18,753,000 to the seller (after the 1.10% fee), and $38,000 to the intermediary, if involved.

Target: $209,000 GETH revenue, settlement in hours not weeks

Reporting & Analytics

All participants access deal history via dashboard, including a digital passport with ESG data and a complete audit trail.

Target: complete audit trail for compliance
